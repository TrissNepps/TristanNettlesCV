University of North Florida, 2015

In January 2013, I transferred to the University of North Florida where I continued my studies in biology and in particular, coastal biology. Research conducted during my time at UNF centered on the role of viral protein 30 in the transcription of the Ebola Virus. This research was conducted under the tutelage of Dr. Michael Lentz, the department head of Molecular and Cell Biology at UNF. As well as my coursework I also founded and organized the first 3D Printing club in North Florida. UNF3D became the second largest student organization, behind only Student Government, on university campus while I served as President. I graduated from the University of North Florida in July 2015.

Florida State College at Jacksonville, 2012

I attended Florida State College Jacksonville from August of 2011 until December of 2012 in order to complete the required coursework for an Associates of Science Degree. Upon conferment of this degree, I applied for and received admittance to the University of North Florida where I began work towards my Bachelors of Science in Biology.
